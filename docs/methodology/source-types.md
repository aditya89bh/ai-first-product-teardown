# Acceptable and Unacceptable Source Types

The [evidence hierarchy](evidence-hierarchy.md) grades *how strong* a piece of evidence is.
This document decides *what may be used as a source at all*. A source can be admissible but
weak (low tier), or inadmissible regardless of how compelling it seems. Inadmissible sources
never enter the library, even at E5.

## Acceptable sources

- **First-hand observation** of the product under a recorded unit of analysis, with enough
  detail to reproduce.
- **Official primary material**: the maker's documentation, changelogs, help centre,
  pricing pages, official blog posts, and on-the-record statements.
- **Reputable independent reporting** that cites checkable evidence and has editorial
  standards.
- **Peer-reviewed or preprint research**, weighted by venue and scrutiny.
- **Public, terms-compliant artefacts**: publicly available screenshots, recordings, or
  documents that were lawfully published and that we may cite with attribution.
- **Corroborated public user reports** from identifiable public venues, used as E4/E5 and
  only with corroboration.

## Unacceptable sources

These are never admissible, at any tier:

- **Fabricated or unverifiable material** — anything we cannot, in principle, trace to a
  real source. Inventing a source is a serious integrity violation.
- **Leaked, confidential, or paywalled-circumvented material.** We do not use content
  obtained by breaching confidentiality, security, or access controls.
- **Private communications** shared without the sender's consent.
- **Content requiring terms violations to obtain**, including scraping that breaches a
  service's terms or defeating access restrictions.
- **Undisclosed AI-generated "facts"** presented as sourced information. Model output is not
  a source; it must be verified against an acceptable source before use.
- **Anonymous claims with no venue or corroboration** presented as anything more than an
  open question.

## Handling and provenance

- **Every source is recorded**, even first-hand observation, with enough detail to locate or
  reproduce it (see [citation standard](citation-standard.md)).
- **Archive volatile sources.** Web content changes; capture a dated copy or archive link so
  the evidence remains checkable if the original moves.
- **Attribute quotations.** Third-party quotations are brief, attributed, and used within
  fair-use / fair-dealing limits; long reproduction is not permitted.

## When source admissibility is unclear

If it is unclear whether a source is acceptable — for example, ambiguous terms, or uncertain
provenance — treat it as inadmissible until clarified, and raise it under
[governance](../governance/). It is better to omit a claim than to rest the library's
credibility on a source we cannot stand behind.
