# Evidence Triangulation Rules

Triangulation is the practice of confirming a claim through **multiple independent lines of
evidence**. It is how a claim earns the right to be called a fact or held at High confidence.
A single source — however strong — can be wrong, out of date, or misread; independent
agreement is what makes a finding robust.

## What counts as independent

Two pieces of evidence are independent only if they could plausibly disagree — that is, they
do not share a common origin that would make agreement automatic.

- **Independent:** a first-hand observation *and* the maker's documentation *and* a
  third-party report that each arrive at the same behaviour.
- **Not independent:** three blog posts that all cite the same original source; two
  observations by the same researcher in the same session; a vendor page quoted by several
  outlets. These are one line of evidence wearing different clothes.

Independence is about *provenance*, not count. Ten copies of one claim triangulate nothing.

## The triangulation bar

- **Facts** generally require agreement across **two or more independent lines** of
  evidence, ideally spanning tiers on the [evidence hierarchy](../methodology/evidence-hierarchy.md)
  (for example, observation + primary source).
- **High confidence** requires triangulation; a lone source caps confidence at Moderate (see
  [confidence levels](../methodology/confidence-levels.md)).
- **Behavioural claims** about non-deterministic products require **repetition** as part of
  triangulation: multiple runs, ideally across sessions or accounts, not a single lucky
  result.

## Handling disagreement between sources

When independent sources conflict, that is a finding, not a nuisance:

1. Record the conflict explicitly rather than picking the convenient source.
2. Prefer the stronger tier and the more recent evidence, but state that a conflict exists.
3. If the conflict cannot be resolved, downgrade the claim's confidence and, where
   appropriate, present competing interpretations as separate labelled inferences or
   hypotheses.

Unresolved conflict lowers confidence; it does not get hidden.

## Triangulation and non-determinism

Because AI-first products behave probabilistically, triangulation has a temporal dimension:

- A behaviour seen once is an observation; a behaviour seen consistently across repeated,
  independent trials approaches a fact about the product's *tendencies*.
- Report the frequency and conditions ("observed in 8 of 10 runs on these dates") rather than
  implying determinism.

## What triangulation is not

- It is not averaging away disagreement to reach a tidy answer.
- It is not stacking many weak, dependent sources to simulate strength.
- It is not a substitute for a strong primary source when one is needed for the claim type
  (intent needs primary material; behaviour needs observation).

## Recording

Each triangulated claim cites all the independent records that support it, so a reviewer can
confirm the lines really are independent and really do agree.
