# Contributor Research Ethics

This document sets the ethical standards contributors accept when producing teardowns. It
complements the [code of conduct](../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) (which governs how we treat each
other) by governing how we treat **subjects, sources, and readers**. These standards are not
optional; violating them undermines the library's core promise.

## Duties to the truth

- **Never fabricate.** Do not invent facts, sources, quotes, data, URLs or findings.
  Fabrication is the gravest violation and grounds for removal.
- **Never misrepresent confidence.** Do not present an inference or hypothesis as a fact, or
  weak evidence as strong (see [fact-inference-hypothesis](../methodology/fact-inference-hypothesis.md)).
- **Label synthetic material.** Any illustrative or synthetic example is explicitly marked
  *synthetic*, so it can never be mistaken for observed evidence.
- **Report disconfirming evidence.** Evidence that cuts against your conclusion is included,
  not buried.

## Duties to subjects (the products and their makers)

- **Study fairly.** Analyse the product as it is, under a clearly stated unit of analysis;
  do not strawman it or cherry-pick its worst moment as representative.
- **Do not entrap or misuse.** Do not manipulate a product into producing harmful output for
  the purpose of a "gotcha", and do not publish content that could function as a how-to for
  harm.
- **Respect terms and law.** Do not breach terms of service, defeat security, or access
  private data to obtain evidence (see [source types](../methodology/source-types.md)).

## Duties to sources and third parties

- **Protect privacy.** Do not expose individuals' personal data; follow the
  [privacy policy](privacy-and-sensitive-info.md).
- **Attribute honestly.** Credit sources; do not pass others' work off as first-hand.
- **Handle confidential material correctly.** Do not use leaked or confidential material;
  if you encounter it in the repository, report it per [SECURITY.md](../../SECURITY.md).

## Duties to readers

- **Be transparent about method and limits.** State what cannot be concluded, not only what
  can (see [methodology limitations](methodology-limitations.md)).
- **Disclose conflicts.** Declare relevant [conflicts of interest](bias-and-conflicts.md).
- **Correct promptly.** When wrong, fix it visibly through the
  [corrections process](corrections-and-disputes.md).

## Duties around AI assistance in the research itself

- Any tool used to help draft or analyse is just that — a tool. Its output is **not a
  source** and must be verified against acceptable sources before any claim is made (see
  [source types](../methodology/source-types.md)).
- Contributors remain fully responsible for every claim they submit, regardless of how it was
  drafted. "A tool produced it" is never an excuse for an unverified claim.

## Enforcement

Ethical violations are handled under the [code of conduct](../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) and may
result in reverted contributions, withdrawal of affected teardowns, and removal from the
project. Fabrication and deliberate misrepresentation are treated most seriously.

## Principle

Ethics here is not a compliance layer bolted on top of the research; it *is* the research
standard. A library that cuts ethical corners cannot credibly ask readers to trust its
findings.
