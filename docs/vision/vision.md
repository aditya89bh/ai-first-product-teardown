# Vision and Long-Term Purpose

## Purpose

To build the most rigorous, reusable body of knowledge about *how AI-first products
actually work* — not how they are marketed — so that people who build, fund, design,
study and regulate these products can reason about them from evidence rather than hype.

## The problem we are addressing

The pace of AI product releases has outrun the vocabulary used to evaluate them. Public
discussion tends toward two failure modes:

1. **Demo-driven enthusiasm** — conclusions drawn from a single impressive interaction,
   with no account of reliability, failure modes, or how the product behaves at the
   edges.
2. **Reflexive dismissal** — blanket scepticism that ignores genuine shifts in how work
   gets done.

Both are cheap because neither requires structured evidence. This library exists to make
the disciplined middle path — careful, sourced, reproducible analysis — cheaper to
produce and easier to trust.

## What we are building

A durable research library with three properties:

- **Systematic.** Every product is examined through the same six dimensions using the
  same method, so teardowns are comparable rather than idiosyncratic.
- **Evidential.** Every claim carries a source, a date and a confidence level. The
  boundary between fact, inference and hypothesis is explicit everywhere.
- **Reproducible.** The method is documented well enough that a stranger can repeat an
  analysis, arrive at their own judgement, and file a dispute if they disagree.

## What success looks like

- A product leader can read a teardown and understand not just *what* a product does but
  *what we know, how confidently, and on what basis*.
- A researcher can take our evidence records and re-derive our conclusions — or refute
  them — without contacting us.
- A newcomer can learn a transferable method for evaluating AI-first products, not just
  absorb our opinions about specific ones.

## What this is not

This is not a review site, a ranking, a newsletter, or a marketing channel. It does not
optimise for engagement, novelty or contrarian takes. It optimises for being *correct
and checkable* over a long horizon. Non-goals are stated explicitly in
[`docs/vision/non-goals.md`](non-goals.md).

## Time horizon

We assume a multi-year horizon. Individual product facts age quickly; the *method* for
studying them should not. When products change, teardowns are versioned and updated
(see governance); when the method improves, changes are documented and dated. The
library is designed to remain useful long after any single product covered in it has
changed or disappeared.
