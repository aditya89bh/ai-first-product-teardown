# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

This is a research community. We commit to an environment where contributors of any
background can do careful work, disagree productively, and hold each other to high
evidential standards without fear of harassment or bad-faith attack.

## Standards

Behaviour that sustains the community:

- Critiquing **claims and evidence**, not people.
- Assuming good faith and asking for clarification before assuming error.
- Welcoming disagreement that is specific, sourced and actionable.
- Crediting others' work and disclosing conflicts of interest honestly.
- Accepting correction gracefully when the evidence goes against you.

Behaviour that will not be tolerated:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Deliberately introducing false, fabricated or unsourced claims.
- Misrepresenting others' analysis or arguing in bad faith.
- Sustained disruption of discussion, review or the correction process.

## Research-specific conduct

Because this is an evidence-first library, some conduct expectations are specific to our
work:

- **Do not fabricate.** Inventing facts, sources, quotes or data is the most serious
  violation possible here and is grounds for immediate removal.
- **Do not misrepresent confidence.** Presenting an inference or hypothesis as an
  established fact undermines the entire library.
- **Disclose conflicts.** If you have a relationship with a product under analysis,
  declare it as required by the governance policy.

## Scope

This code applies in all repository spaces — issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions —
and in any public space where an individual is representing the project.

## Enforcement

Violations may be reported by opening a confidential report to the maintainers via the
mechanism described in [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) for sensitive contact, or by flagging a
maintainer directly. Reports will be reviewed promptly and treated confidentially.

Maintainers may take any action they judge appropriate, including warning, temporary
suspension, or permanent removal from the project. Maintainers who do not follow or enforce
this code in good faith may face consequences determined by other maintainers.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted in spirit from widely used open-source community covenants,
with additional research-integrity provisions specific to this project.
